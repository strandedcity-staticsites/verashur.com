The New Gate: A Train Station for Istanbul

Harvard Graduate School of Design
Options Studio
Critic: Hashim Sarkis
2009

How can a new train station connecting the two halves of Istanbul reorganize and reorient the city?

The design for this train station functions as an intermodel urban connector, nested within the surrounding neighborhoods and imposing its own order back onto the city. Like the Grand Bazaar, the station is both a continuation of Istanbulâ€™s streets and an autonomous object within the urban fabric. it is able to project its own identity into the city while remaining embedded within the local scale.

The station makes legible the passenger flows that are collected, reorganized, and diverted to their desired modes of transit. The building acts as an aggregation of filaments that bundle or unravel, based on the meeting or diverging of major passenger corridors. To ensure efficiency and clarity, movement of people is highly controlled up until the moment they are released to the train platforms, at which point the regulated environment gives way to an open space of freedom and informality.
